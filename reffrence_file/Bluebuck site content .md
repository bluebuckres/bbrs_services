### SECTION 5 — FAQ

```
SECTION HEADING: Before you reach out.

Q1: Do you work with early-stage companies, or only post-Series A?
A1: Both — but the fit depends on the project, not the stage. If you have a 
    clear problem, a technical co-founder or strong brief, and you're serious 
    about building right the first time, we'll have a useful conversation. 
    We've shipped MVPs for pre-seed founders and data infrastructure for 
    Series B companies. What matters is whether we're the right team for 
    what you're building.

Q2: We're in the US / UK / Dubai. How does working with a Kolkata-based team actually work?
A2: We've designed our entire process around this. Daily async updates, 
    structured weekly standups at your timezone, and you always speak 
    directly to the engineers building your product — not a project manager 
    translating between you and them. Our team overlaps with both US East 
    Coast mornings and UK afternoons. It works because we've made it work 
    for 40+ international projects.

Q3: We have an internal engineering team. Can you work alongside them?
A3: Yes — and we prefer it. We've embedded as an external AI/ML team for 
    companies with existing engineering, worked alongside CTOs as a specialist 
    layer, and handed off cleanly documented code when we're done. No lock-in, 
    no empire-building. Your team stays in control.

Q4: Can we see past client work that's under NDA?
A4: On a call, yes. We can walk through architecture, decisions, and outcomes 
    for projects we can't name publicly. We won't share client code or 
    screenshots without permission — but we can give you enough to know 
    whether our work is the right standard for yours.

Q5: How many projects do you take at once?
A5: Three to four per quarter, maximum. When we take your project, it gets 
    full team attention — not a shared pool. If we're at capacity when you 
    reach out, we'll tell you immediately and give you an honest timeline. 
    We'd rather lose a project than deliver a bad one.
```
